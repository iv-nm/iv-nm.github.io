Wake Word Detection

Date: 2024-08-09

One of the main challenges I faced so far, was how to streamline the process of detecting when the assistant should start listening.

Method 1: Google speech recognition

Initially, I used Google Speech Recognition for speech-to-text (STT) and PyAudio for text-to-speech (TTS), but I quickly realized this approach introduced significant delays. Vosk, another option I tried, was faster, but I still experienced delays of about 3 seconds before the command execution even began.

That happens because determining any speech from the constant flow of sound is time-consuming, and it can lead to false positives, where Jarvis mistakenly thinks the speech is addressed to him.

Method 2: Wake Word Detection

Wake word detection is a method where a voice assistant listens for a specific word, known as the "wake word," to activate. Instead of constantly analyzing every sound, the system stays idle until it hears the wake word, which triggers it to start listening for commands.

By using Picovoice, I can detect the wake word in just 30ms, making the process faster and more reliable than traditional speech-to-text methods.
